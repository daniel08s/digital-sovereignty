Reference · Storage · builds on Lesson 7

Shopping sketch: simple NAS + optional compute box

Decided direction: keep the NAS as boring storage (no GPU, no transcoding). Optionally add a second machine later for Jellyfin/Immich work — reusing the Crosshair VII there, not as the disk farm.

Architecture: split NAS = Cart S Compute later = Cart C (Crosshair)

Why this split is strong

The NAS stays quiet, full of disks, and easy to reason about (ZFS + shares). Apps that burn CPU/GPU (transcode, Immich ML) land on a separate host that can mount the library over the LAN. Failure domains stay clean: rebuild apps without touching the pool.

Where the Crosshair should go

Prefer: hold the ROG Crosshair VII Hero for the future compute box (Cart C). Put a quieter, simpler board in the NAS (Cart S). Gaming boards are noisy and non-ECC; they are a better match for “occasional media brain” than for a 24/7 disk chassis.

Avoid: building the NAS on the Crosshair “for now,” then hoping to move that board to compute later. Migrating a live ZFS pool to new hardware is doable but stressful — better not to trap your reuse candidate under six spinning disks.

Cart S — Simple NAS (buy / build now)

Job: RAIDZ2 pool, SMB/NFS shares, snapshots. Jellyfin may read files from here later, but this box does not transcode. Direct-play clients only from the NAS’s point of view.

Apps on Cart S?

OS decision still comes next. Pattern that matches this architecture: NAS runs storage (+ maybe lightweight shares). Jellyfin / *arr / Immich prefer Cart C when it exists. Until then you can direct-play from the NAS with a tiny Jellyfin and transcoding disabled — or wait and only stand up media apps on compute.

Cart C — Compute / transcode (later, reuse Crosshair)

Job: Jellyfin (with HWA when needed), Immich ML, *arr stack. Media files live on Cart S; this box mounts them (SMB/NFS) or talks to the NAS API. No big HDD farm required here.

Spare parts still sidelined

FX-6100 + 970A: not Cart S or Cart C. GTX 650: drawer. WD Blue 1 TB: scratch only — never the RAIDZ2 vdev.
